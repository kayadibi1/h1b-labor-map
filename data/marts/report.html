The two regime changes that shape your odds

1. Beneficiary-centric selection (effective FY2025)

USCIS now treats each unique beneficiary, not each registration, as a single lottery entry per employer. This eliminated the gaming where one candidate could be registered by 5+ shell employers. FY2026 was the second cycle under this rule; the published selection rate was 35% (118,660 selections out of 336,153 eligible beneficiaries).

2. Wage-weighted lottery (effective Feb 27, 2026 — applies to FY2027+ registrations)

The new rule grants more lottery entries to higher-wage offers: Level IV = 4 entries, Level III = 3, Level II = 2, Level I = 1. Combined with beneficiary-centric selection, the effective per-level selection rate for the FY2027 lottery (which ran in March 2026) looks like this:

When to apply

Cap-exempt employers — anytime

Universities, affiliated nonprofits, nonprofit research orgs, government research orgs can file at any point in the year. No cap, no lottery, no March deadline. You can convert from OPT to H-1B as soon as a petition is approved.

Action: outreach now → throughout 2026.

Cap-subject employers — March cycle

Next registration window: March 2027 (FY2028 lottery). Employers register candidates in mid-March; USCIS conducts the weighted random selection; petitions filed April–June; H-1B status starts October 1, 2027.

Action: outreach by December 2026 so the employer has time to do internal sponsorship sign-off, prevailing-wage determination, and registration before the March window.

The full timing_calendar view in /data/marts/timing_calendar.csv has a per-employer line you can join into your outreach tracker.

Methodology — how realism is computed

Sources (verified live 2026-05-27)

  • DOL OFLC LCA disclosure — FY2022–FY2025 Q4 xlsx, 437,834 certified rows. Headline employer-attestation signal (intent).
  • USCIS H-1B Employer Data Hub — FY2020–FY2023 CSVs, ~209K rows. Initial Approvals = "new sponsorship" headline; Continuing is renewal context only.
  • BLS OEWS May 2024 metro — wage benchmarks by SOC × CBSA.
  • IPEDS HD 2023 — Title-IV institutions, the HIGH-confidence HIGHER_ED cap-exempt seed.
  • SEC EDGAR — public-company CIK / ticker for parent-subsidiary entity resolution.
  • Census/OMB CBSA — county-to-metro crosswalk.
  • WARN Act — NY/MA/VA/DC scraping; layoff signal.

Sponsorship realism formula

Cap-exempt (no lottery):
realism = approval_rate × min(1, approvals/3) × (1 − staffing) × (1 − layoff)

Cap-subject (FY2027+ wage-weighted lottery applies):
realism = approval_rate × Σ(pct_levelL × lottery_rateL) × min(1, approvals/10) × (1 − staffing) × (1 − layoff)

Why LCA-only rankings are misleading

  • An LCA is a wage attestation, not a petition. Employers file speculative or bulk LCAs that never become petitions.
  • USCIS Hub measures actual petition decisions, but mixes Initial (new) and Continuing (renewal) — only Initial matters for an OPT-to-H-1B job seeker.
  • Cap-subject petitions also need to win the lottery to matter. Pre-2025 selection rates inflate badly under naive averaging.

Known gaps

  • Geographic coverage is curated (DC/NYC/Boston/SF/San Jose/Seattle). Off-map rows have cbsa_code = null. Expand via geo_review.csv.
  • USCIS Hub publishes ~2 FYs behind DOL (latest is FY2023). The most recent DOL filings (FY2024–FY2025) will have uscis_initial_approvals_window = null because USCIS hasn't published yet.
  • WARN scrapers exist for NY/MA/VA/DC; CA/TX/WA/NJ/IL/FL/GA are JS-gated and need per-state Playwright work.
  • PERM (green-card) data is ingested but not yet wired into the join — green_card_friendly_employers view is empty.
